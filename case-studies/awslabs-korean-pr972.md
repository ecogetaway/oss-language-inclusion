# Case Study: awslabs/data-on-eks — Korean — PR #972

## Repository
[awslabs/data-on-eks](https://github.com/awslabs/data-on-eks)

## Contribution Type
Pull Request

## Language
Korean (ko)

## What Happened
A contributor submitted a Korean i18n support PR to AWS Labs' data-on-eks project. The maintainer responded with reasonable feedback — asking the contributor to split the PR into smaller pieces for easier review. This is a legitimate and constructive request. However, no further maintainer engagement occurred after the initial feedback. The PR was subsequently bot-closed for inactivity after approximately 45 days — while the contributor was presumably still working on the requested split.

## Timeline
- Opened: 2025-2026
- Maintainer feedback: Split into smaller PRs
- Closed: ~45 days after opening
- Days open: ~45

## Closing Reason
Bot-stale — closed while contributor was likely acting on maintainer feedback

## Maintainer Response
Initial feedback only — no follow-up after requesting split

## Why This Matters
This case illustrates a subtle but important failure mode: a maintainer who engages constructively but then disappears. The contributor received guidance — split the PR — and was presumably doing exactly that when the stale bot closed the contribution. Without a structured review workflow that pauses stale timers when a maintainer has requested changes, contributors who follow instructions get penalised. Good faith on both sides is not enough without infrastructure to support the workflow.
